ABOUT KABARET

Kabaret is a show or staging art that comes from the Western World where there is usually entertainment, music, comedy and execution of theatrics or dances. The main difference between cabaret and other performances is the venue-a restaurant or a nightclub with a stage of the show and its audience sitting impressively on tables (while eating or drinking) and watching the show. The place itself is also called "cabaret". In the turn of the 20th century, there was a major change in the cabaret culture. The dancers included Josephine Baker and the Brazilian transvestite dancer João Francisco dos Santos (aka Madame Satã). Cabaret shows can range from political satire to light entertainment, each by a master of ceremony (MC), or join the event.
